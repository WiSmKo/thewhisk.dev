About Me.

I’m Birmingham born and raised but I also have Indian & German heritage. Growing up with my Indian Oma, we would frequently recieve guests from all over the world and I was fortunate enough to get to visit many of them in return. Therefore, from a young age, I have had the oppurtunity to nurture my inquisitiveness from a global perspective.

I have been fascinated by computers for as long as I can remember, nagging my parents to teach me more than the school cirruculum had on offer and teaching myself what I could from free internet resources. On the other hand, I also have a great love for arts and music. My weekends as a teenager were spent at youth theare school. By the time I was reaching the end of my Diploma in Computing I was becoming quite involved with my local music scene, at the time they seemed like two completely different worlds that didn’t have much in common.

The last 5 years for me have laregly been defined by music, I have worked locally as a performer, band manager and event organiser. Having had the oppurtunity to play shows across the country and to contribute to a flourishing local scene I feel incredibly privaliged for the experiences I have been a part of. However as my need to promote music and events through digital means grew, my passion for technology and development was reignited. I went full circle, enthused by my two alternate universe’s coliding I have strived to stay on top of the constantly evolving world of development through online courses and kept my mind challenged with regular practice of maths and related subjects.

In my spare time I like to keep active and read. Cycling, indoor climbing, fitness and nutrition were significant past times/interests for me in 2020. I make time to read everyday, I can’t get enough of a good book and the pile ‘to read’ grows faster than I can get through them. Currently I’m reading Tolstoy’s novel ‘Anna Karenina’ & the poplar science book ‘Why We Sleep’ by neuroscientist Matthew Walker.
